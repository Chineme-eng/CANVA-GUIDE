1. Licensing Essentials

Selling digital products made in Canva is legal, but you must follow the Transformation Rule. You cannot simply take a single piece of Canva stock media and sell it.

Asset Type Free User Pro User
Templates Allowed (Must be unique) Allowed (Template Link Only)
Stock Photos As part of a design Limited to 480k total pixels unedited
AI Content Free use rules apply Pro use rules apply
Crucial: Never sell "Standalone" elements. If a customer can extract a single Pro icon or photo from your product without it being part of a larger design, you are in violation.

2. Custom Size Master Chart (2026)

Using the right dimensions ensures your product doesn't look blurry (pixelated) when printed or viewed on high-res tablets.

Product Category Dimensions (px/in) Optimized For
Digital Planners 2480 x 3508 px iPad / GoodNotes (A4)
Phone Wallpapers 1284 x 2778 px iPhone 14-17 Pro Max
Standard Printables 8.5 x 11 in US Letter (Home Printers)
Wall Art (2:3 Ratio) 24 x 36 in Large Posters / Framing
Etsy Listing Thumbnails 2700 x 2025 px 4:3 Search Grid
The 2X Scaling Rule: For digital products meant for high-resolution screens (like planners), design at double the intended size. This allows users to zoom in with a stylus without seeing pixels.

3. Saving & Export Secrets

How you save your file determines if it’s usable or "theft-proof."

The "Flatten" Method

When exporting a PDF Print for a finished product (like an E-book), always check Flatten PDF. This merges all elements into one layer, preventing users from opening your PDF in Illustrator and stealing your graphics.

Color Profiles (CMYK vs RGB)

  • RGB: Best for digital planners, social media, and wallpapers. Colors are vibrant.
  • CMYK: Essential for anything meant to be printed (invitations, posters). Prevents "muddy" color shifts.

Clipart & Stickers

Always export as PNG at 3.0x Size with Transparent Background checked. This gives the customer a high-density file they can resize without losing quality.

4. The Delivery PDF Protocol

You never send a raw Canva link via email. You send a Delivery PDF. This is your customer's "unboxing" experience.

Delivery PDF Checklist:
  • A high-res "Mockup" image of the product.
  • A bold button labeled "CLICK HERE TO ACCESS TEMPLATE".
  • The Canva Template Link (Share > Template Link).
  • A link to a 1-minute "How-to" video or PDF guide.
  • Support contact info & License terms.

5. Legal & Trademarks

One of the most misunderstood areas of Canva design. Pay close attention to these rules:

Logo Trademarks

You cannot trademark a logo that uses Canva stock elements. To create a trademarkable logo for a client, you must use only basic lines/shapes or upload your own original vector art.

The "Client" Transfer

If you design for a specific client (not a mass-market digital product), you can transfer the design to them, but they are still bound by Canva's licensing terms regarding the Pro elements inside.

7. Accessibility Standards (WCAG)

Accessibility is no longer optional. Accessible digital products rank higher in search algorithms and reach a significantly wider customer base.

  • Readability: Keep body text at 14pt minimum.
  • Color Contrast: Use the Canva Accessibility Checker app. If your design fails the "Contrast" test, adjust your palette until it passes the 4.5:1 ratio.
  • Flat Text: Avoid using heavy "Text Effects" (like Curve or Glitch) for critical information, as screen reading software struggles to parse them correctly.
